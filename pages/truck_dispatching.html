Course Overview

This course balances foundational knowledge with practical, repeatable workflows. It teaches you the terminology & law you'll need to operate (US DOT, MC, FMCSA basics), then moves quickly into real dispatch activities: finding freight, building rates, negotiating, using TMS & load boards, compliance checks, creating paperwork, and handling exceptions.

What you will learn (detailed)
  • Industry roles: carriers, brokers, shippers, 3PLs, freight brokers, factoring companies
  • How to read rate confirmations, bills of lading, carrier packets
  • Using load boards effectively & intel-sourcing (sourcing lanes, deadhead calculation)
  • Calculating line-haul, fuel surcharge, accessorials, detention
  • Communication scripts for driver/carrier/broker interactions with templates
  • Complying with FMCSA rules, ELD usage, HOS calculations
  • Setting up a dispatch business: client onboarding, contracts, pricing models, bookkeeping & KPIs
  • Handling claims, detention disputes, freight exceptions
  • Tools & tech: freight matching platforms, simple TMS, ELD portals, Google Sheets templates & automation ideas

Syllabus — Modules & Lessons

Module 1 — Dispatch Fundamentals (4 lessons)
  1. Welcome & industry overview — terms & parties
  2. Paperwork 101 — BOLs, rate confirmations, carrier packets
  3. Regulatory basics — FMCSA, DOT numbers, insurance & authority
  4. Communication & documentation best practices
Module 2 — Load Finding & Rate Building (6 lessons)
  1. Load boards overview — what to use & when
  2. Lane research — how to determine realistic rates
  3. Calculating deadhead and fuel impact
  4. Building a rate sheet & margin model
  5. Negotiation templates and price anchoring
  6. Exercise: price 5 sample loads
Module 3 — Carrier Compliance & Safety (5 lessons)
  1. Reading carrier safety profiles (CSA scores)
  2. ELD basics & Hours-of-Service (HOS) rules
  3. Insurance, authority verification, and MCS-150
  4. Driver qualification files (DQ files)
  5. Practical compliance checklist for onboarding a carrier
Module 4 — Tools, Templates & Tech (5 lessons)
  1. TMS basics & free/paid options
  2. Google Sheets templates for quick quoting & routing
  3. Using mapping & ETA tools to plan trips
  4. Email & call templates (onboarding, booking, follow-ups)
  5. Automation ideas with Zapier/Make (optional)
Module 5 — Business of Dispatching (4 lessons)
  1. Pricing models: percentage vs flat-fee
  2. Contracts & service agreements
  3. Client acquisition & retention strategies
  4. KPIs, bookkeeping & scaling your team
Module 6 — Capstone & Troubleshooting (3 lessons)
  1. Capstone project: full dispatch from lead to settlement
  2. Common issues — claims, delays, detention resolution
  3. Final exam & certificate
